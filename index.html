WE ARE IN IT FOR THE LONG RUN

THE WORLD HAS A 2 BILLION TONS PER YEAR TRASH PROBLEM

By 2050 it'll be 3 billion tons per year.

Do you ever wonder what happens to the trash after it leaves your hands?
You might be surprised to hear that the Snickers wrapper you threw out will long outlive you, sitting somewhere on earth for hundreds if not thousands of years. Sometimes, it ends up in animals. A lot of other times, it ends up in the ocean, inside of a turtle, a sea lion, or a whale. Trash is not a part of any animal's diet, so more often than not, these animals die in agony.
It's just one Snickers wrapper, you may say. But...

SHOULD WE TAKE A PAUSE AND REEVALUATE OUR CHOICES?

If we want to treat the cause rather than the symptoms of the waste crisis, we need to reevaluate a lot of our choices. And it starts with reducing and reusing. Anything we cannot reuse, we will recycle.
Reduce and reuse can begin with your household and go all the way to the manufacturers of products.
As people, today, we can start not just reducing, reusing, and recycling, but also using our greatest weapon, our voice. Educating and raising awareness is contagious - in a good way.
